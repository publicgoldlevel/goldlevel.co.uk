# Set 1 — Clarity Map / Meaning

## John Doe synthetic scenario

John Doe is the founder of **SignalNest AI**, a B2B SaaS tool for surfacing missed follow-ups from recorded meetings. The team has traffic, demos, and friendly comments, but prospects do not understand whether the tool is analytics, task management, compliance, or coaching.

## 1. What are you trying to move forward?

I am trying to move forward the homepage and demo-request path for SignalNest AI. We have a working product and early customer conversations, but the offer page is not producing enough qualified demo requests from the traffic and warm referrals we already have.

## 2. Where is it not converting, deciding, sequencing, or landing clearly?

People understand that “meeting intelligence” sounds useful, but they do not understand what they are supposed to do next or what specific problem we solve first. The homepage explains the product architecture before naming the business consequence. Prospects say, “Interesting,” then ask whether this is a meeting summarizer, CRM, compliance dashboard, or team coaching product.

## 3. What made this important now?

We have a partner webinar in 11 days and a list of 420 registrants. If the page stays vague, we will push traffic into a leaky doorway and waste the highest-intent audience we have had so far.

## 4. Is there a live offer, project, page, decision, buyer conversation, launch, deadline, or opportunity attached?

Yes — live homepage, demo-request CTA, partner webinar, and three enterprise buyer conversations scheduled this month.

## 5. What link, page, post, pitch, message, or artifact should be looked at first?

Homepage draft: signalnest.example/home  
Demo CTA section: signalnest.example/demo  
Recent enterprise reply: “This is promising, but I cannot tell whether it belongs to RevOps, HR, or the COO.”

# Set 2 — Custom Moment Map / Coherence

## John Doe synthetic scenario

John Doe runs **Studio Cartographer**, a creator education brand with a newsletter, a paid workshop, a Notion template, and a coaching offer. Each part has some traction, but there is no coherent path from audience attention to purchase.

## 1. What are you trying to move forward?

I am trying to turn my audience and product pieces into one coherent buying path. I have a newsletter, a low-ticket template, a paid workshop, and a coaching offer, but they feel like separate doors instead of one progression.

## 2. Where is it not converting, deciding, sequencing, or landing clearly?

People reply to essays and say the ideas help them, but when I announce the workshop, they hesitate or ask what the difference is between the template, the workshop, and coaching. I think the parts are good, but the sequence is confusing.

## 3. What made this important now?

I plan to open the workshop again next week. Last launch had strong replies and weak sales. I do not want to run another launch that generates admiration but not commitment.

## 4. Is there a live offer, project, page, decision, buyer conversation, launch, deadline, or opportunity attached?

Yes — live workshop launch next week, current newsletter audience, existing template sales page, and a decision about whether to lead with workshop or coaching.

## 5. What link, page, post, pitch, message, or artifact should be looked at first?

Workshop page: studiocartographer.example/workshop  
Template page: studiocartographer.example/template  
Recent newsletter launch post: “Build your map before your next move.”

# Set 3 — Custom Moment Card / Method

## John Doe synthetic scenario

John Doe is the operator of **Northline Labs**, a small AI implementation studio. The business has demand, but delivery is scattered. Every client engagement becomes custom, and the team cannot repeat the same diagnostic-to-pilot process twice.

## 1. What are you trying to move forward?

I am trying to turn our AI implementation work into a repeatable diagnostic-to-pilot method. Right now every client starts differently, every audit becomes custom, and delivery depends too much on my personal attention.

## 2. Where is it not converting, deciding, sequencing, or landing clearly?

Qualified prospects are interested, but they stall when we describe the engagement because it sounds too open-ended. Internally, the team does not know what comes first: data inventory, workflow mapping, stakeholder interviews, or prototype design.

## 3. What made this important now?

We have four warm prospects and one signed client waiting for kickoff. If we do not standardize the first move, we will create delivery drag before the work even starts.

## 4. Is there a live offer, project, page, decision, buyer conversation, launch, deadline, or opportunity attached?

Yes — signed client kickoff next Monday, four warm prospects, and a decision about the first repeatable diagnostic step.

## 5. What link, page, post, pitch, message, or artifact should be looked at first?

Current proposal deck: northline.example/ai-pilot-deck  
Kickoff checklist draft: northline.example/kickoff-v3  
Client email asking, “What exactly happens first?”

# Set 4 — Visual API Ledger / Seven Language Skills

## John Doe synthetic scenario

John Doe leads **LedgerBridge Advisory**, a fractional growth consultancy. The team runs outreach and posts content, but their internal reporting confuses attention with proof. They cannot tell whether a campaign produced market signal or just noise.

## 1. What are you trying to move forward?

I am trying to make our outreach and content feedback loop measurable enough to decide what to repeat. We need a proof ledger for our sales conversations, not just a list of impressions and positive replies.

## 2. Where is it not converting, deciding, sequencing, or landing clearly?

We keep calling things “traction” when they may only be attention. A LinkedIn post gets saves, a DM gets praise, and a call gets interest, but we do not track which signals lead to paid commitment, referrals, or concrete next steps.

## 3. What made this important now?

We are deciding whether to double down on a positioning angle for the next 30 days. If we use the wrong metric, we may scale the wrong message.

## 4. Is there a live offer, project, page, decision, buyer conversation, launch, deadline, or opportunity attached?

Yes — current outreach campaign, next 30-day positioning decision, and eight recent sales conversations that need interpretation.

## 5. What link, page, post, pitch, message, or artifact should be looked at first?

Outreach tracker: ledgerbridge.example/outreach-sheet  
Recent post: ledgerbridge.example/post/positioning  
Sales notes folder: ledgerbridge.example/sales-notes
