# Set 1 — Clarity Map / Meaning

## Buyer details

1. Name: John Doe  
2. Email: john.doe+clarity@example.com  
3. Business / project name: SignalNest AI  
4. Website, offer page, profile, or relevant link: signalnest.example/home  
5. Preferred deadline or urgency: Need the map within 48 hours because the partner webinar is in 11 days and the page needs a decisive edit before traffic goes live.

## A. The live object

6. What is the one offer, project, page, or decision you want mapped?  
The SignalNest AI homepage and demo-request path.

7. Who is it for?  
Revenue, customer success, and operations leaders at B2B companies with 50–500 employees who lose accountability after meetings.

8. What outcome are you trying to create?  
A qualified demo request from people who recognize that missed follow-ups, unclear ownership, and meeting drift are costing execution speed.

9. What is currently moving, if anything?  
Referral traffic, webinar registrations, warm enterprise conversations, and positive comments on the product concept.

10. Where is it not converting, deciding, sequencing, or landing clearly?  
The page creates curiosity but not commitment. Buyers cannot tell which department owns the problem, what risk is reduced, or why they should request a demo now.

## B. Trigger and timing

11. What made this important now?  
The webinar will create the first real traffic spike, and three enterprise prospects are likely to revisit the page before deciding whether to continue.

12. Is there a launch, deadline, opportunity, buyer conversation, paused offer, repeated non-result, or public post attached?  
Yes — partner webinar, three enterprise conversations, and a decision about the homepage before launch.

13. What happens if this remains unresolved for another 30 days?  
We may misread weak conversion as weak product demand when the actual problem is unclear meaning and trust framing.

14. What would clarity allow you to do that you cannot currently do?  
Choose one buyer problem, rewrite the hero and demo CTA, brief the webinar host, and stop explaining the product from four different angles.

## C. Evidence and attempts

15. What have you already tried?  
We rewrote the hero three times, added feature bullets, added a product diagram, and moved testimonials higher.

16. What did those attempts not resolve?  
They did not clarify what problem the buyer should recognize in the first five seconds.

17. What feedback, objections, silence, sales data, audience response, or traction signal have you received?  
Average time on page is decent, referrals say it sounds useful, but demo requests are low. Prospects ask, “Is this for RevOps, HR, or leadership?”

18. Paste the relevant page, pitch, offer, DM, post, deck, notes, or links.  
Homepage: signalnest.example/home  
Demo section: signalnest.example/demo  
Pitch line: “AI-powered meeting intelligence that turns conversations into operational accountability.”  
Buyer reply: “Useful, but I cannot tell where this lives inside the company.”

19. What do you suspect the problem is?  
We are naming the mechanism before naming the cost of the problem.

20. What should be checked carefully?  
Whether the page needs one buyer owner and one cost-of-status-quo frame before product explanation.

## D. Constraint field

21. Which feels closest?  
Buyer does not understand the value.

22. Where do you feel the most drag?  
The first screen and the transition from “interesting product” to “I should request a demo.”

23. What are you currently compensating with?  
More explanation, more feature bullets, more diagrams, and longer sales calls.

## E. Decision use

24. What decision do you want to be able to make after receiving the map?  
Which buyer problem and department should own the homepage message for the webinar push.

25. What would make this diagnostic worth the $300?  
A clear diagnosis of the message break and one edit path we can implement before the webinar.

26. What would make it not worth doing?  
If the output is generic copy advice rather than naming the actual constraint.

27. Any hard constraints I should preserve?  
Do not imply surveillance. Do not overpromise AI accuracy. Keep enterprise trust and privacy concerns visible.

## F. Permission and boundaries

28. May I quote or anonymize any part of this later as proof if useful?  
Ask first.

29. Is anything confidential or not for future use, even anonymously?  
Enterprise prospect names, traffic numbers, and product roadmap details.

30. I understand this is a diagnostic artifact, not a guarantee of revenue, conversion, or business outcome.  
Yes.

3. Business / project name: Northline Labs

5. Preferred deadline or urgency: Need a repeatable first move before Monday’s kickoff.

## A. The live object

6. What is the one offer, project, page, or decision you want mapped?  
The first step of our AI implementation diagnostic.

7. Who is it for?  
Operational leaders at service businesses who want AI automation but do not know which workflow to start with.

8. What outcome are you trying to create?  
A repeatable diagnostic entry point that reduces custom scoping and makes the first week of delivery predictable.

9. What is currently moving, if anything?  
We have one signed client, four warm prospects, and internal team capacity.

10. Where is it not converting, deciding, sequencing, or landing clearly?  
Prospects want help, but the engagement sounds too custom. Internally, we do not know whether to begin with data, workflows, stakeholder interviews, or prototype design.

## B. Trigger and timing

11. What made this important now?

12. Is there a launch, deadline, opportunity, buyer conversation, paused offer, repeated non-result, or public post attached?

13. What happens if this remains unresolved for another 30 days?  
Delivery will become founder-dependent, scope will sprawl, and we will keep selling implementation before diagnosing the first constraint.

14. What would clarity allow you to do that you cannot currently do?  
Give the team a repeatable kickoff card and tell prospects exactly what happens first.

## C. Evidence and attempts

15. What have you already tried?  
We created a readiness checklist, a workflow audit, a data inventory, and a prototype menu.

16. What did those attempts not resolve?

17. What feedback, objections, silence, sales data, audience response, or traction signal have you received?  
Prospects ask, “What do we need to prepare?” and “How do you decide what to automate first?” The team asks the same questions internally.

18. Paste the relevant page, pitch, offer, DM, post, deck, notes, or links.

19. What do you suspect the problem is?

20. What should be checked carefully?  
Whether the first artifact should be a workflow constraint card rather than a broad audit.

## D. Constraint field

21. Which feels closest?  
Execution is scattered.

22. Where do you feel the most drag?  
First-week delivery and prospect confidence before payment.

23. What are you currently compensating with?  
More calls, more custom scoping, more checklists, and more founder explanation.

## E. Decision use

24. What decision do you want to be able to make after receiving the map?  
What the first repeatable diagnostic step should be for every AI implementation client.

25. What would make this diagnostic worth the $300?  
A one-card method we can use Monday and reuse with prospects.

If it expands into a full operating model instead of narrowing the first move.

27. Any hard constraints I should preserve?

4. Website, offer page, profile, or relevant link: ledgerbridge.example/outreach-sheet  
5. Preferred deadline or urgency: Need the map before the next 30-day positioning decision.

## A. The live object

6. What is the one offer, project, page, or decision you want mapped?  
Our outreach and content proof loop for the fractional growth advisory offer.

7. Who is it for?  
B2B service founders doing $500k–$3M who need sharper positioning and sales process clarity.

8. What outcome are you trying to create?  
A decision about which message to repeat for the next 30 days based on proof, not internal preference.

9. What is currently moving, if anything?  
LinkedIn engagement, DM conversations, booked calls, and several warm replies.

10. Where is it not converting, deciding, sequencing, or landing clearly?  
We do not know which signals matter. The team treats likes, saves, replies, calls, and payments as the same kind of traction.

## B. Trigger and timing

11. What made this important now?  
We are about to choose the next campaign angle. If we choose based on vanity metrics, we may scale the wrong message.

Yes — 30-day campaign planning deadline and eight recent sales conversations to interpret.

Separate resonance from commitment and decide what to repeat, stop, or test next.

15. What have you already tried?  
Tracking impressions, saves, comments, DM replies, booked calls, call notes, proposals sent, and closed revenue.

16. What did those attempts not resolve?  
The dashboard collects data but does not tell us which language is producing qualified buying motion.

17. What feedback, objections, silence, sales data, audience response, or traction signal have you received?
