### What is the one offer, project, page, or decision you want mapped?

### Who is it for?

Revenue, customer success, and operations leaders at B2B companies with 50–500 employees who lose accountability after meetings.

### What outcome are you trying to create?

Qualified demo requests from buyers who recognize that missed follow-ups, unclear ownership, and meeting drift are costing execution speed.

### What is currently moving, if anything?

Referral traffic, webinar registrations, warm enterprise conversations, and positive comments on the product concept.

### Where is it not converting, deciding, sequencing, or landing cleanly?

## B. Trigger and Timing

### What made this important now?

The webinar will create the first real traffic spike, and three enterprise prospects are likely to revisit the page before deciding whether to continue.

### Is there an upcoming launch, deadline, opportunity, buyer conversation, stalled offer, repeated failure, or public post attached?

Yes. Partner webinar, three enterprise conversations, and a decision about the homepage before launch.

### What happens if this remains unresolved for another 30 days?

We may misread weak conversion as weak product demand when the real problem is unclear meaning, trust, and role ownership.

### What would clarity allow you to do that you cannot currently do?

Choose one buyer problem, rewrite the hero and demo CTA, brief the webinar host, and stop explaining the product from four different angles.

## C. Evidence and Attempts

### What have you already tried?

We rewrote the hero three times, added feature bullets, added a product diagram, moved testimonials higher, and added a security note.

### What did those attempts fail to resolve?

They did not clarify what problem the buyer should recognize in the first five seconds or who inside the company should own the decision.

### What feedback, objections, silence, sales data, audience response, or traction signal have you received?

Average time on page is decent, referrals say it sounds useful, but demo requests are low. Prospects ask, “Is this RevOps, HR, or leadership?”

### Paste the relevant page, pitch, offer, DM, post, deck, notes, or links.

Homepage: signalnest.example/home. Demo section: signalnest.example/demo. Pitch line: “AI-powered meeting intelligence that turns conversations into operational accountability.” Buyer reply: “Useful, but I cannot tell where this lives inside the company.”

### What do you suspect the problem is?

We are naming the mechanism before naming the cost of the problem.

### What are you worried I might miss?

That this is not just a copy issue. It may be a buyer ownership issue, a trust issue, and a timing issue all showing up as copy confusion.

## D. Constraint Field

### Which feels closest?

Buyer does not understand the value.

### Where do you feel the most drag?

The first screen and the transition from “interesting product” to “I should request a demo.”

### What are you currently compensating with?

More explanation, more feature bullets, more diagrams, longer sales calls, and extra follow-up emails.

## E. Decision Use

### What decision do you want to be able to make after receiving the map?

Which buyer problem and department should own the homepage message for the webinar push.

### What would make this diagnostic worth the $300?

A clear diagnosis of the message break and one edit path we can implement before the webinar.

### What would make it not worth doing?

If the output is generic copy advice rather than naming the actual constraint.

### Are there any hard constraints I should not violate?
