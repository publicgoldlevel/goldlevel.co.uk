OMISSION — When silence becomes negligence or a crime

No. 01·visual systems map·760×1074

Summary

A visual investigation of omission as a governance failure: when silence, delay, or non-response stops being neutral and starts carrying public consequence.

What it maps

Responsibility gaps, delayed acknowledgement, institutional silence, and the boundary between absence of action and accountable harm.

Why it matters

Public systems often fail not only through visible misuse, but through unaddressed gaps. This piece makes the inactive part of accountability legible.
