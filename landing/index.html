01 · Page logic

What is the page trying to do?

A public page is not just text. It is a sequence of decisions: what the reader sees first, what they believe next, and whether the page gives them enough reason to continue.

Promise

The value the page is making visible.

Reader

The person the page is actually speaking to.

Proof

The evidence that makes the promise believable.

Action

The next step the page needs to make obvious.

02 · Friction

Where does trust drop?

Most pages do not fail because everything is wrong. They fail because one part is unclear, missing, overexplained or in the wrong order.

Value

What is not yet obvious enough.

Risk

What makes the reader hesitate.

Weight

What is taking too long to understand.

Gap

What the reader still needs before acting.

03 · First correction

What should change first?

The read does not try to solve the whole business. It identifies the first public-facing changes that would make the asset clearer, stronger and easier to act on.

Cut

Remove what weakens the path.

Move

Put the strongest point earlier.

Add

Bring forward the missing proof.

Clarify

Make the next step simpler.
