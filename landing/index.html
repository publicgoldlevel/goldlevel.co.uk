Protocol

The read follows a governed conversion path.

The point is not to summarise everything. The point is to identify what the material is, what it is not, what can safely be claimed, and what should be built next.

  1. 01

    Ingest complexity

    Read the submitted material as a system, not a pile of fragments.

  2. 02

    Name what it is / is not

    Separate artifact type, audience, purpose, non-purpose, and scope.

  3. 03

    Structure the material

    Build a clean outline, hierarchy, section logic, and usable route.

  4. 04

    Separate evidence states

    Mark what is known, inferred, unproven, unsafe, or not yet ready.

  5. 05

    Map release boundaries

    Decide what belongs private, trusted-context, public, or not exported.

  6. 06

    Return next-step artifact

    Deliver the clean read and a short action map for the next move.

FAQ

Practical answers before you send.

What does “governed” mean here?

It means the read separates what is known, inferred, unproven, unsafe to claim, private, public, and ready to build next.

Can I send AI-generated text?

Yes. AI outputs are a strong fit when they need source-status separation, claim safety, structure, or a next-step artifact.

Do you rewrite the material?

Bronze and Silver focus on clarity and structure. Gold can include a short rewritten extract or execution board.

What should I not send?

Do not send highly sensitive legal, medical, financial, or third-party private data. Redact where needed. Keep the first pass bounded.
