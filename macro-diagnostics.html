Primary-data macro substrate

E4 macro annotation

UN-backed SDG verification indicates the wider system remains far off track: about 35% of assessed SDG targets are on track or making moderate progress, roughly 48% are moving too slowly, and about 18% have regressed since 2015. More than 800 million people remain in extreme poverty; 2024 exceeded 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels on an annual basis; forcibly displaced people have surpassed 120 million, nearly double the 2015 level; and only about 45% of people believe political systems are responsive. Data capacity has improved, but remains fragile: roughly 70% of SDG indicators now have sufficient coverage, all 234 indicators have agreed methodologies, yet key goals including gender, cities, climate, and institutions remain under-covered, and funding concentration leaves measurement exposed.

What that means

  • The system backdrop is materially stressed before any AI narrative is added on top.
  • Reception conditions are weak: institutions, publics, and labour systems are already under load.
  • Measurement improved, but under-coverage and funding concentration still limit interpretive stability.
  • The macro substrate should be treated as a condition of reception, not as decorative context.
Interpretive overlay

S/C model overlay

Within that legitimacy-poor and measurement-fragile backdrop, the AI map is best read as a concentration topology rather than a fully certified live-market ledger. Compute capacity, capital routing, cloud dependence, and narrative control are thickening in parallel. The visible conflict around public operators can function as a compression surface for wider system contradictions rather than a complete explanation of those contradictions.

This is an interpretive read, not a primary-data claim.

Operational rule

  • Keep reversible: do not write the AI field as a closed moral verdict.
  • Keep selective: do not let one visible face stand in for the whole system.
  • Keep human-fit: public reception follows lived pressure, not abstract technical elegance.
  • Keep bounded: do not let an infographic imply full market certification where only topology is supported.
