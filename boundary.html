Boundary

The read stays bounded on purpose.

The first pass is designed to reduce confusion, not turn the issue into something heavier than it needs to be. Scope discipline is what makes the output usable.

Why bounded is better

Useful output comes from constraint, not from sprawl.

A read that stays within its scope is more useful than one that expands to fill the available space.

Grounded in what you send

The read stays close to the page, offer, thread, notes, or voice note in front of it.

What gets named

You get the main issue, what is weakening trust or clarity, and what to change first.

What it avoids

No vague padding, no inflated certainty, and no forced dependency beyond what is useful.

Why it helps

It keeps the work readable, usable, and tied to one clean next step rather than endless interpretation.

Scope boundary

The boundary is part of the value.

Constraint is not a limitation added after the fact. It is one of the reasons the return stays useful.
