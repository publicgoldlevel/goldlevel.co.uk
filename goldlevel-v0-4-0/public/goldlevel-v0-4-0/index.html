Ten-layer website pass
I

Surface Expression

Public-facing form layer: visible objects/elements, signals carried, available patterns, visible outcomes.

Site use: Reads what the market can see before any claim about internals is made.

II

Field Layer

Context, environment, latent possibility space, boundary conditions.

Site use: Defines the containing frame so the asset is not diagnosed in abstraction.

III

Selection Layer

Noise-to-signal, governed inclusion/exclusion, framing, attention gate.

Site use: Controls what enters the map and what remains outside current scope.

IV

Operator Layer

Selection → transformation → interpretation → responsibility → proof demand.

Site use: Defines the functional operator without ego inflation and assigns consequence.

V

Invariant Channels

Leading symmetry, constraints, invariant channels, conservation, residue/structure.

Site use: Names what remains stable across versions, attempts, silence and objections.

VI

Radar / Calibration

Calibration, confidence, false-signal detection, pattern strength, uncertainty.

Site use: Prevents aesthetic coherence from being confused with market proof.

VII

Basin Topology Activation

Basin depth, transient paths, recurrent core, multi-basin ecology, attraction pull.

Site use: Maps the landscape of pull, stability and flow.

VIII

Governance and Constraints

Permission gates, ethics, trajectory, constraint memory, pause, repair, termination.

Site use: Decides what can continue, what must slow, and what must stop.

IX

Consequence and Cost Layer

Time, attention, repair, opportunity, reputational and compounding costs.

Site use: Makes every path pay its cost before escalation.

X

Proof and State Promotion

Receipts, tests, artifacts, measured results, state promotion; no promotion without proof.

Site use: Moves interpretation into validated state only when evidence exists.
