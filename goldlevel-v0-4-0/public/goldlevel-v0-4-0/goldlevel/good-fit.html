Good fit

Use this when the work exists but the read on it is still unstable.

The page does not need to be broken to need a read. Often the pressure sits in category confusion, thin proof, weak trust translation, or too much noise around a decision.

Three common reasons to book

Recognise the signal before you send.

If one of these describes the situation, the diagnostic route is likely the right first move.

Offer not landing

The promise is real, but the buyer cannot classify it quickly enough to trust it or pay.

Buyer hesitation

The page is calm and polished, but proof, audience fit, or method visibility are still too thin to convert cleanly.

Decision fog

There is too much live material or too many interpretations to see the next move clearly.

Not a good fit

When this route does not apply.

Being clear about where the route does not fit prevents poor outcomes for both sides.

Nothing live yet

If there is no live material to read, there is nothing to ground the diagnostic in.

Regulated professional advice

This route does not substitute for legal, medical, financial, or therapeutic professional advice.

Ongoing retainer work
