Good Fit

Where the diagnostic route fits. Where it does not.

Read this before booking. The route works well in specific situations. It is not suited to every kind of request, and saying so keeps the service honest and useful.

Three common situations

Reality not reading clearly

The work, situation, or signal exists, but it is being misread, misframed, or not understood in its actual form.

Trust not holding

The material may be serious, but the basis for confidence is still too thin, too obscured, or too weakly translated to support commitment.

Too much live complexity

There is too much in motion, too many interpretations, or too little hierarchy for the next step to become clear.

When this route does not apply.

Being clear about where the route does not fit prevents misapplication, overreach, and weak outcomes.

Nothing sufficiently live to read

If the material, situation, or signal has not yet taken enough form, there is nothing reliable to ground the diagnostic in.

The matter belongs to regulated judgment

Where licensed, clinical, legal, financial, or emergency authority is required, this route is not a substitute.

The need is for continuity, not a read

If the situation requires sustained stewardship, repeated intervention, or ongoing embedded support, this route is too bounded for that function.
