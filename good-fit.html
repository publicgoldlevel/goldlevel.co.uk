Good fit

Use this when the work exists but the read on it is still unstable.

The page does not need to be broken to need a read. Often the pressure sits in category confusion, thin proof, weak trust translation, or too much noise around a decision.

Good fit

Three common reasons people book.

Recognise the signal before you send.

If one of these describes your current situation, this service is likely the right first move.

Offer not landing

The promise is real, but the buyer cannot classify it quickly enough to trust it or pay for it.

  • People engage but do not convert
  • You are explaining it differently each time
  • The offer is real but the language is still unstable

Buyer hesitation

The page is calm and polished, but proof, audience fit, or method visibility are still too thin to convert cleanly.

  • Interest is present but conversion is slow
  • People ask the same clarification questions
  • Trust signals are missing or buried

Decision fog

There is too much live material or too many interpretations to see the next move clearly.

  • Multiple options feel equally valid
  • The situation has too many live layers
  • You need a bounded outside view before acting
Not a good fit

When this service does not apply.

Being clear about what this service is not prevents poor outcomes for both parties.

Nothing live yet

If there is no material to read — no page, no offer document, no thread, no notes — there is nothing to ground the diagnostic in. Build the material first.

Regulated professional advice

This service does not substitute for legal, medical, financial, or therapeutic professional advice. Those situations need a regulated professional.

Ongoing strategy retainer

This is a bounded one-pass service, not a weekly retainer or ongoing strategic partnership. It is designed for a specific stuck point, not continuous support.
