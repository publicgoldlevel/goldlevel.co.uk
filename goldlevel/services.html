Input, return, and boundary

What can be sent, what comes back, and how the read stays bounded on purpose.

Input & ReturnBoundary
What can be sent

Material with a live decision attached to it.

  • Pages or documents where trust or clarity is weakening.
  • Offer notes that exist but do not yet land cleanly.
  • Threads or message paths where the issue sits in communication.
  • Voice notes or short briefs when speaking is easier than writing.
What comes back

A bounded written return.

  • The main pressure named as clearly as possible.
  • What seems solid versus what is weakening the situation.
  • What to change first or where to look next.
  • A readable output, not an open-ended engagement.
